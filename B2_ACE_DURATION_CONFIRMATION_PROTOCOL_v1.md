## Ruling and provenance

The requested-duration sanity criterion is now backbone-aware:

`abs(decoded_duration_seconds - requested_duration_seconds) <= 0.25 seconds`

The boundary is inclusive. ACE-Step v1 and Stable Audio 3 both use the same
0.25-second tolerance. This changes only the old exact decoded-frame check;
sample rate, channel count, decodeability, finite samples, non-silence,
adjacent provenance, and no-clobber checks remain unchanged. Stable Audio 3
receives no new call under this protocol and its previously exact outputs are
unaffected in practice.

The amendment is grounded in the SHA-bound old-repository ledger extraction in
`provenance/b2/ace_duration_quantization_provenance_v1.json`: eight independently
seeded ACE-Step v1 requests targeting 30.0 seconds each decoded to 1,435,551
frames at 48 kHz, or 29.9073125 seconds. The retained first B2 call is separately
re-adjudicated without mutation in
`provenance/b2/ace_call0_duration_readjudication_v1.json`.

## Gates before the model call

The committed config and authorization template are inert. A production call
requires all of the following to pass before adapter invocation:

1. A post-amendment `DECISIONS.md` entry whose latest assignments authorize
   only this confirmation and keep the original two-call authorization closed.
2. A completed external authorization valid for no more than 24 hours and
   binding the live decision hash, every package hash, exact prior evidence,
   exact source/checkpoint/environment identities, exact job, caps, node, and
   physical GPU.
3. Clean `main` with `HEAD == origin/main == authorization.origin.commit`.
4. Exact GNU timeout parentage: 1,800 seconds with a 30-second kill-after.
5. One visible A800 on an12 or an29, TP1, one replica, at least 60,000 MiB free,
   no neighboring compute process, and at most 5% observed utilization. No
   process is preempted or killed.
6. Re-hashing and semantic validation of all retained first-call evidence and
   both committed duration-provenance records.
7. Exact checkpoint-tree and clean upstream-source verification immediately
   before the exclusive per-call claim.

The requested clip cap remains 30 seconds. The amended sanity rule may accept
an observed decoder-quantized duration up to 30.25 seconds; this does not
authorize a request longer than 30 seconds.

## Terminal rule

Successful external-authorization validation first creates a distinct durable
authorized-attempt claim, before prior-evidence, runtime, placement, GPU, or
adapter preflight. From that consumption boundary onward, any failure is
terminal and non-retryable. The later global execution and per-call claims are
also durable and new. Any new model, engine, retention, sanity, deadline, or
post-call validation failure is terminal
`BLOCKED_ON_ENGINEERING_FAILURE`, consumes this one-call authority once claimed, and receives
no retry. A passing row must record measured NFE, wall time, peak allocated and
reserved VRAM, file/provenance hashes, exact decoded duration, absolute duration
error, and the 0.25-second policy result. Only a terminal PASS may support a
subsequent decision declaring ACE-Step v1 `MEASURED_READY` and opening a fresh
ACE-only core queue.

No audio is generated by preparing or testing this package.
